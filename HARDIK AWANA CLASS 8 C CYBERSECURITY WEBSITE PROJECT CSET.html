SAFETY TIPS AGAINST CYBERCRIME





  1. Secure Logins: Use unique passwords for every account and always enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and college portals.
  2. Public Wi-Fi/Charging: Avoid using public Wi-Fi or USB charging ports in cafes or metros for sensitive tasks; they are common targets for data theft.
  3. QR Code Rule: Never scan a QR code to receive money. Scammers use this trick to drain your account instead of paying you.
  4. Social Privacy: Set profiles to "Private" and never share your location, phone number, or school name publicly to avoid cyberstalking.
  5. Emergency Contact: If you are scammed or harassed, immediately call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930 or report it at

    National Cybercrime Report Page

CYBER LAWS IN INDIA






Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act):
The foundational law, enabling e-commerce, recognizing electronic records/signatures, and establishing offenses.

IT (Amendment) Act, 2008:
Expanded the IT Act, introducing new offenses (like publishing private images) and increasing penalties.

Indian Penal Code (IPC):
Sections of the IPC (e.g., cheating, forgery, defamation) are applied to cybercrimes.

IT Rules, 2011 (and subsequent) :
Detailed rules for implementing the IT Act, covering intermediaries, digital signatures, etc.

Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act):
Focuses specifically on processing personal data, balancing individual privacy with data fiduciary needs.

National Cyber Security Framework (NCSF):
Outlines national strategies for cybersecurity.







Core Concepts & Offences under IT Act, 2000/2008





Legal Recognition:
Validates electronic records, digital signatures, and e-governance.

Cyber Crimes & Penalties:-

  • Tampering/Hacking (Sec 66): Unauthorized access/damage to computer systems.

  • Identity Theft (Sec 66C): Fraudulent use of IDs.

  • Cheating by Impersonation (Sec 66D): Using communication devices for fraud.

  • Publishing Private/Obscene Content (Sec 66E/67): Violating privacy or posting explicit material.

  • Offensive Messages (Sec 66A - struck down by Supreme Court): Intended to cause annoyance.



Data Protection: Addresses data security and breach notification.








PHISHING





Phishing is a cyber attack where hackers try to trick people into sharing personal information like:

  • Passwords
  • Bank details
  • OTPs
  • Credit / debit card numbers


They usually pretend to be trusted companies such as banks, schools, or social media platforms.





How to Protect Yourself from Phishing



Never click unknown links



Do not share OTP or passwords



Check the sender’s email carefully



Use strong passwords



Report suspicious messages










MALWARE





Malware, (short for "malicious software") refers to any intrusive code or program intentionally designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system, network, or data.






Core Malware Types



  • Ransomware: Encrypts files and demands a ransom payment for the decryption key. Modern "multi-extortion" strains also threaten to leak stolen data if demands aren't met.
  • Viruses: Malicious code that attaches to legitimate programs and requires human action (like opening a file) to execute and spread.
  • Worms: Standalone programs that self-replicate and spread automatically across networks without any human intervention.
  • Trojans: Disguise themselves as legitimate software to trick users into installing them, often creating "backdoors" for further attacks.
  • Spyware: Secretly monitors user activity to steal sensitive data like passwords, credit card numbers, and browsing habits.
  • Adware: Automatically displays or downloads unwanted advertisements, often tracking user behavior to serve targeted (and sometimes malicious) ads.







CYBERBULLYING
AND
CYBERSTALKING





Cyberbullying and cyberstalking are forms of online harassment that involve using digital platforms to target, intimidate, or harm individuals.





Key Differences


Cyberbullying: Repeatedly sending or sharing harmful, false, or mean content to embarrass or humiliate a person. It is most common among minors and often revolves around emotional manipulation.


Cyberstalking: A pattern of persistent, unwanted online attention that induces fear or concern for personal safety. It frequently involves surveillance, tracking location, or threats.
